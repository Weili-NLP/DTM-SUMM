[1] Summary can be generated through extractive methods by ranking and ordering sentences with the aim of maximum coverage, significance, cohesion and minimum redundancy. [2] Generating non-Redundant and more coherent summary is the goal in multi-document summarization (MDS). [3] MMR approach (Maximal Marginal Relevance) is proposed to reduce redundancy. [4] Summaries can be generic, query-focused, timeline summarization and update summarization. [5] DualSum and concept-based ILP methods are proposed for update summarization considering the updates regarding the topic . [6] LexRank, LexPageRank, Biased-LexRank and Markov Random Walk Model (MRW) are Graph-based methods to weight, rank and select sentences. [7] Statistical feature based approaches deal with features like sentence position, centrality, resemblance, relative length which to extract important sentences and words from source text. [8] Ouyang proposed an approach in which Regression models are applied for ranking the sentences in query-based summarization of multiple documents. [9] Bayesian topic models labeling sentences or words with topics and selecting sentences that are closely related with query to generate Topic Aspect Oriented Summary. [10] Candidate summaries are compared with human-generated reference summaries by Pyramid and ROUGE measure to evaluate overall summary quality on DUC and TAC conference data set.
