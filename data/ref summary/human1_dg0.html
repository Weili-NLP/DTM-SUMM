[1] Automatic text summarization system generates a short length summary that maximizes coverage, coherence, salience and non-redundancy . [2] Summary can be generated through extractive as well as abstractive methods. [3] Extractive summaries try to extact important sentences, achieve more coherent and less redundant summaries. [4] Objectives are coverage of information, information significance, redundancy and cohesion in text. [5] Redundancy is one of the biggest problems in MDS. [6] MMR approach (Maximal Marginal Relevance) is used for reducing redundancy. [7] Maximum Coverage and Minimum Redundancy (MCMR). [8] Bayesian sentences-based topic model for query-focused summarization, every sentences is associated to a latent topic and each document has a topic distribution. [9] An unsupervised summarization model for generic text as an Integer Linear Programming problem (ILP) identifies important sentences from the document and consists of the relevant content of the entire document. [10] In the conferences of DUC and TAC, summaries are evaluated by Pyramid method and ROUGE on redundancy, focus, grammaticality, referential clarity, and structure and Coherence.
