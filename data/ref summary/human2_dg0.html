[1] Extractive summarization try to select important sentences, achieve more coherent and meaningful summaries. [2] Objectives are coverage of information, information significance, redundancy and cohesion in text. [3] Bayesian topic models such as BayeSum and TopicSum are used in summarization. [4] The centroid-based method is one of extractive summarization methods. [5] MEAD is an implementation of the centroid-based extractive summarization. [6] Query-focused summarization requires combining query based relevance with information novelty when generating summaries. [7] MMR approach (Maximal Marginal Relevance) is suggested for reducing redundancy. [8] Graph-based: LexRank, LexPageRank, Biased-LexRank and Markov Random Walk Model (MRW) are used to weight and rank sentences. [9] A bi-gram or concept based ILP framework is proposed for selecting sentences by optimizing salience and redundence in extractive summarization. [10] Performances on DUC and TAC data sets are evaluated by Pyramid method and ROUGE, like ROUGE-N, ROUGE-L,ROUGE-W, ROUGE-S and ROUGE-SU.
